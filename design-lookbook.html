The brief

Make the design mean probability.

The app already looks clean and competent — a calm Khan-Academy skin: warm paper, one teal, card lists, an eyebrow-and-title rhythm. The problem isn't polish, it's identity: drop the logo and it could be any tidy edtech course. Nothing on screen says probability, and the product's whole thesis — predict, then watch thousands of trials settle onto the truth — never shows up as a visual idea. These directions fix that by building everything around one signature and a two-color story.

  • Signature motif — the convergence line. A jagged running average settling onto the dashed "truth" rule. It's literally what "the long run" means (the Law of Large Numbers) and it becomes the hero, the progress chart, and the lesson result.
  • A two-color story with meaning. Teal = the truth the simulation reveals; amber = your prediction. The same pairing reads on every page, so color carries the core loop instead of decorating it.
  • Numbers get their own typeface. A tabular monospace for every stat, percentage and prediction — this is a numbers app, so the figures should look engineered, not generic.
  • Ink "observatory" surfaces. A deep navy replaces the flat teal hero block — it frames the glowing data and lets teal + amber pop.
  • Layout shifts: Home leads with a live demo, Overview becomes a learning path, Profile a lab "ID card", and the Lesson makes predict-vs-verify the visual subject.

Use the Dark toggle (top-right) to check both themes — every token is mirrored. Everything below is a static mock; nothing here is wired into the app yet.

The one memorable thing

The convergence line

One motif carries the brand: a noisy running average converging onto the truth as trials pile up. Teal is the value the long run reveals; the dashed amber line is the guess you committed first. You'll see this exact shape in the hero, in progress, and in every graded result.

Law of large numbers

10,000 coin flips

Early on, the average swings wildly. Given enough trials it settles — that's the whole course in one picture.
